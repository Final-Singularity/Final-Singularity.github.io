About Me

Hi, I'm Apoorv. I've been a part of Samsung Semiconductor India R&D as a Hardware-Software Co-Design Engineer for the past three years. My area of expertise is SoC RTL and micro-software Design, specifically developing clock & power management IP. I have worked on Samsung's Exynos mobile chips and Generative AI Accelerator SoCs.

The scientific breakthrough that I am looking forward to the most is the development of a synthetic brain. I am curious about the intricate circuitry and learning mechanisms of the brain and the implications it can have on modern edge AI devices. My research interest broadly involves everything that can contribute towards this ultimate goal - neuro-inspired AI, neuromorphic computing, hardware-efficient learning algorithms, efficient computer architecture and computational neuroscience.

I am an alumnus of IIT Bombay where I spent five memorable years earning a Dual Degree (B.Tech and M.Tech) in Electrical Engineering. My prior research experience has been in neuromorphic algorithms and biomimetic computational neuroscience under the supervision of Prof. Udayan Ganguly

Art has been a constant influence throughout my life. I view art as the ultimate source of expression, learning and connection with others. I often find myself dancing and painting whenever I'm in need of indulgence. I occasionally enjoy reading as well, my favorite non-fiction being the "Paradox of Choice" by Barry Schwartz. I like to connect with other people and learn from their perspective. You can reach out to me over mail or text for a hearty discussion over any of the above topics.
